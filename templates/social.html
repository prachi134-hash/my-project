Social Work Activities

Empowering society through volunteerism, sustainability, and community development

The National Service Scheme (NSS) is a Central Sector Scheme of the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports, Government of India, introduced in 1969. It aims to develop the personality and character of student youth through voluntary community service.

Objective: Develop the personality and character of student youth through voluntary community service.

Ideological Orientation: Inspired by Mahatma Gandhi's ideals, NSS follows the motto "Not Me But You".

Activities Include:

  • Skill Development Activity in Villages
  • Vaccination Drive
  • National Flag Distribution under “Har Ghar Tiranga”
  • Cloth Donation Drive
  • Voting Registration Camp
  • Health Checkup Camp
  • Blood Donation Camp
  • Street Plays on Environment and Social Awareness
Prof. Anand Deshkar - NSS Program Officer, CCOEW, Nagpur

The Green Club promotes environmental awareness, sustainability, and eco-friendly practices. Activities include tree plantation drives, campus clean-ups, and awareness workshops. Students actively contribute to reducing the ecological footprint and creating a greener environment.

Key Activities:

  • Tree Plantation Drives on campus and nearby communities
  • Clean-up Campaigns
  • Workshops on waste segregation and sustainable living
  • Environmental Awareness Programs
  • Collaboration with NGOs and Rotary Club for conservation projects

Recent Event: A tree plantation drive in collaboration with NSS and Rotary Club involved 15 students from Deaf & Dumb Industrial School, Shankar Nagar, Nagpur, along with Principal, management, and faculty.

Unnat Bharat Abhiyan (UBA) connects students with rural communities to implement sustainable solutions in education, health, sanitation, and livelihood. Students participate in fieldwork, research, and social innovation projects.

Health Check-up Camp: Organized for villagers of Mandva, Katangdhara, Pipaldhara, and Nagazari in collaboration with Janakalyankari Samiti, Nagpur and Swami Vivekanand Medical Mission, Nagpur. 134 villagers benefited, including 64 women. Seven students volunteered for the event.

School Kit Distribution Program: On 30th May 2024, 100 school kits were distributed to students in Mandva and Pipaldhara villages. Each kit contained notebooks, pens, pencils, scales, erasers, sharpeners, sketch pens, and other stationery items.

Cummins College is attached to No 2 Mah. Air Sqn. NCC, Nagpur. It is an open unit for Nagpur University students. Cadets undergo three academic years of training including drills, ground service training, rifle shooting, camps, national-level programs, and social activities.

Training & Activities:

  • Drill, Ground Service Training, Rifle Shooting
  • Participation in National Level Camps and Adventure Activities
  • Theory exams preparation and B & C certificate examinations
  • Community service and environmental awareness programs

Students Enrolled & Achievements: Some students include Karina Kaur, Ritika Kumari (All India Vayu Sainik Camp 2024), Aarya Karkhanis, Sanika Puranik, Renuka Moharir (EBSB 2 OTA Kamptee), Harshita Mankar (Scuba Diving Camp Mumbai), and others. Cadets have won awards and certifications for exemplary participation.
