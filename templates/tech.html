IEEE

This prestigious event was organized by IEEE. The IEEE Minor Series 1.3, held on September 18, 2024, featured a crucial Freshmen Induction Session by Coding Ninjas, in partnership with the Cummins College of Engineering for Women, Nagpur. The IEEE Club organized a debate competition titled "Minor Series 1.0 - Debate" on 25th July 2024. The event took place at Gargi Seminar Hall and commenced at 3:00 PM, drawing significant interest from students and faculty alike.

IETE

The Institution of Electronics and Telecommunication Engineers (IETE) student chapter at Cummins College of Engineering for Women, Nagpur aims to promote technical knowledge and professional development in the field of electronics, telecommunications, and IT. Activities conducted include technical workshops, seminars, guest lectures by industry experts, and hands-on projects to strengthen practical knowledge. Regular guidance sessions help students prepare for competitive exams and certifications in the electronics and IT sector. IETE also organizes inter-college technical competitions such as circuit design challenges, robotics contests, and paper presentations to encourage innovation and practical implementation of theoretical concepts.

CSI

The Computer Society of India (CSI) student chapter at Cummins College of Engineering for Women, Nagpur is dedicated to the development of computing and IT skills among students. CSI organizes workshops, coding competitions, hackathons, and seminars to expose students to the latest trends and technologies in computer science and information technology. Members also participate in research-based projects, collaborative learning sessions, and industrial visits to gain real-world exposure. CSI promotes networking and knowledge-sharing among students, faculty, and industry professionals, helping students build a strong foundation in software development, AI, data science, and cybersecurity.

ASM

The ASM (American Society for Metals) student chapter at Cummins College of Engineering for Women, Nagpur focuses on metallurgy, materials science, and mechanical engineering disciplines. ASM organizes technical sessions, material testing workshops, expert lectures, and hands-on projects to enhance students’ understanding of metals, alloys, and industrial applications. Members are encouraged to engage in research projects, internships, and collaborative industrial visits to gain practical knowledge of materials engineering. The chapter also provides opportunities to attend national and international conferences, competitions, and competitions related to mechanical and materials engineering.

Industry 4.0 Technocrat Club

The Industry 4.0 Technocrat Club at Cummins College of Engineering for Women, Nagpur is focused on emerging technologies including IoT, AI, robotics, and smart manufacturing. The club conducts workshops, hands-on projects, and seminars on Industry 4.0 technologies. Students participate in competitions, hackathons, and innovation challenges to develop practical skills and problem-solving capabilities in real-world scenarios. This club serves as a platform for students to collaborate with industry experts, gain insights into future technologies, and prepare for careers in advanced manufacturing, automation, and smart systems.
